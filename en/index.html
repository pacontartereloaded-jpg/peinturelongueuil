Why us

The detail that separates amateur work from real craftsmanship.

Our clients rarely call us for paint. They call us because they want a wall as smooth as glass, a perfect cut line where the ceiling meets the wall, and a color that turns out exactly like in the magazine.

  • Surface preparation — sanding, joint taping, caulking, the right primer. Nothing gets painted until it's ready.
  • Premium paints — Benjamin Moore, Sico, Farrow & Ball. Low-VOC options for children's rooms.
  • A stable, trained team — no surprise subcontracting; the same painters from start to finish.
  • Complete protection — furniture, floors, light fixtures. You get your home back exactly as it was, only better.
  • Schedules respected — start date and completion date written into the contract, and honored.

Frequently asked

The questions we hear at every visit.

How much does a painter cost in Longueuil?
The price depends on surface area, prep work needed, and paint type. For a standard condo, expect $2.50 to $4.50 per square foot painted. Our free on-site estimate gives you a fixed contract price — no billing surprises.
Do you have an RBQ license?
Yes. We hold a valid license from the Régie du bâtiment du Québec and carry $2 million in liability insurance. The license number is on every contract.
Do you handle drywall finishing and repairs?
Yes — it's actually one of our most-requested services. Good painting starts with a perfect wall. We do plastering, sanding, caulking and primer application before any finish coat.
How long does a typical project take?
A two-bedroom condo usually takes 2–3 days. A full house, 5–8 days depending on prep. We set start and completion dates in the contract — and honor them.
Do you work outside business hours for commercial spaces?
Absolutely. For offices, clinics and retail spaces, we schedule work for evenings and weekends. Your operations don't stop.
What areas do you serve?
All of Longueuil: Vieux-Longueuil, Saint-Hubert, Greenfield Park. We also work in Brossard, Boucherville, Saint-Lambert and across the South Shore of Montreal.
